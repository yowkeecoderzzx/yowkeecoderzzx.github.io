VIGAN LONGGANISA

Vigan longganisa is a unique Filipino pork sausage originating from Vigan City, Ilocos Sur. It's known for its distinct garlicky, salty, and slightly tangy flavor, unlike the sweeter traditional longganisa. The sausages are small, plump, and traditionally enjoyed for breakfast with fried eggs and rice, often dipped in Ilocos vinegar with chili, garlic, and onions.

UBE HALAYA

Ube halaya or halayang ube (also spelled halea, haleya; from Spanish jalea 'jelly') is a Philippine dessert made from boiled and mashed purple yam (Dioscorea alata, locally known as ube).[1] Ube halaya is the main base in ube/purple yam flavored-pastries and ube ice cream. It can also be incorporated in other desserts such as halo-halo. It is also commonly anglicized as ube jam, or called by its original native name, nilupak na ube.[2]

BANIG

A banig is a traditional handwoven mat, primarily used for sleeping and sitting in the Philippines. It's made from natural materials like pandan, buri (palm), or tikog (reed grass) leaves, which are dried, dyed, and then woven into intricate patterns. Banigs are a staple in Filipino homes and are also used in various cultural and traditional activities.
